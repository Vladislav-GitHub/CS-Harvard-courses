Dr. Richard P. Feynman

“Perhaps one day we will have machines that can cope with approximate task descriptions, but in the meantime, we have to be very prissy about how we tell computers to do things.”

One of the greatest minds of humanity

Here's a time line of Dr. Richard's P. Feynman life:

  • 1918: Born in New York City
  • 1935 - 1939: Obtained his B.Sc in the MIT
  • 1941 - 1942: Feynman started work on the atomic bomb project at Princeton University
  • 1942: He received his doctorate from Princeton
  • 1943 - 1945: Feynman continue work on the atomic bomb project at Los Alamos
  • 1945: Feynman was appointed as a professor of theoretical physics at Cornell University
  • 1950: Feynman accepted a position as professor of theoretical physics at the California Institute of Technology
  • 1965: He was awarded the Nobel Prize jointly with Schwinger and Tomonoga:

    "For fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics, with deep-ploughing consequences for the physics of elementary particles."

  • 1979: Feyman's health had deteriorated and he had surgery for stomach cancer.
  • 1986: His final major task was as a member of a committee set up to investigate the cause of the explosion on the space shuttle Challenger
  • 1988: Dies at the age of 69
